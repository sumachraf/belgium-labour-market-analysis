# Belgium Labour Market Analysis

## Overview
This project analyses employment gaps in the Belgian labour market using open data from Eurostat and Statbel (Statistics Belgium). The central question: what structural barriers stand between Belgium and its stated goal of 80% employment by 2029?

## Key Findings
- Non-EU born workers in Belgium have an employment rate roughly 15 percentage points lower than native-born workers (2024)
- Belgium ranks 2nd in Europe for the largest native vs non-EU born employment gap
- The gap has persisted since 2015 with no sustained improvement
- Belgium's native-born employment rate sits below the EU27 average and below all direct neighbours
- Education significantly raises employment rates across all Belgian provinces, but a structural gap remains

## Tools
- SQL (SQLite / DB Browser) — data exploration and analysis
- Power BI — dashboard and visualisation

## Data Sources
- Eurostat Labour Force Survey: Employment by country of birth (lfsa_ergacob)
- Eurostat Labour Force Survey: Employment by migration status (lfsa_erganedm)
- Statbel HVD: Employment rate of people aged 20 to 64
- Statbel HVD: Unemployment rate of people aged 15 to 74
